# 🎀 Practical Cheerleading Challenges - Real-World Math

## 🎯 Overview

All challenges are now modeled after **real-world, practical scenarios** that cheerleaders actually encounter! Each problem teaches algebra through concrete, relatable situations.

---

## 📚 THE 6 CHALLENGE TYPES

### 1. 💰 **TEAM SHOPPING TRIP** (Linear Equations)
**Inspired by**: Shopping & Budgeting

**Real-World Scenario**:
> "Coach gave you $45 to buy hair bows for the squad! Each bow costs $7 at the cheer store. How many can you buy? How much money will you have left?"

**Algebra Taught**: `7x ≤ 45`
- Division to find maximum items
- Multiplication to find total spent
- Subtraction to find change

**Example**:
- Budget: $45
- Price per bow: $7
- Answer: 6 bows (costs $42), $3 change left
- Algebra: `7 × 6 = 42`, `45 - 42 = 3`

**Why It Works**:
- ✅ Concrete numbers (money, items)
- ✅ Real shopping scenario
- ✅ Practical outcome (change left over)
- ✅ Teaches budgeting + algebra

---

### 2. 📊 **PRACTICE PERFORMANCE** (Multi-Step Equations)
**Inspired by**: Sports & Fitness (Performance Tracking)

**Real-World Scenario**:
> "In the FIRST HALF of practice, you nailed 12 perfect tumbling passes! By the END of practice, you had 27 total perfect moves! How many did you do in the SECOND HALF?"

**Algebra Taught**: `12 + x = 27`
- Subtraction to isolate variable
- Understanding parts vs. whole

**Example**:
- First half: 12 moves
- Total: 27 moves
- Answer: 15 moves in second half
- Algebra: `27 - 12 = 15`

**Why It Works**:
- ✅ Tracking real performance
- ✅ Concrete counts
- ✅ Motivating (seeing progress)
- ✅ Teaches part-whole relationships

---

### 3. 🎯 **FUNDRAISER GOAL** (Inequalities)
**Inspired by**: Shopping & Budgeting with constraints

**Real-World Scenario**:
> "The squad needs to raise AT LEAST $80 for new uniforms! You're selling candy bars for $5 each. How many do you need to sell to reach your goal?"

**Algebra Taught**: `5x ≥ 80`
- Finding minimum value
- Understanding "at least"
- Division with ceiling

**Example**:
- Goal: $80 minimum
- Price per candy bar: $5
- Answer: At least 16 candy bars
- Algebra: `80 ÷ 5 = 16`

**Why It Works**:
- ✅ Real fundraising scenario
- ✅ Teaches inequality (≥)
- ✅ Practical goal-setting
- ✅ Motivating (team needs uniforms!)

---

### 4. 🏆 **COMPETITION SCORING** (Systems of Equations)
**Inspired by**: Sports & Fitness (Performance Tracking)

**Real-World Scenario**:
> "You just competed! Your team scored 35 points from stunts. Your TOTAL score was 62 points. Each dance section is worth 9 points. How many dance sections did you complete?"

**Algebra Taught**: `35 + 9x = 62`
- Two-step solving
- Subtraction then division
- Understanding score breakdown

**Example**:
- Stunt points: 35
- Total: 62
- Dance points per section: 9
- Answer: 3 dance sections
- Algebra: `62 - 35 = 27`, `27 ÷ 9 = 3`

**Why It Works**:
- ✅ Real competition scoring
- ✅ Two variables (stunts + dance)
- ✅ Concrete point values
- ✅ Teaches systems thinking

---

### 5. 🏔️ **PYRAMID POWER** (Exponents)
**Inspired by**: Video Games (Scoring/Levels) with multipliers

**Real-World Scenario**:
> "You're building a 3-level pyramid! The BASE level has 3 cheerleaders. Each level UP doubles the difficulty points! What's the TOTAL difficulty at the top level?"

**Algebra Taught**: `3 × 2² = 12`
- Exponential growth
- Repeated multiplication
- Understanding powers

**Example**:
- Base: 3 points
- Levels: 3
- Level 1: 3 points
- Level 2: 3 × 2 = 6 points
- Level 3: 3 × 2 × 2 = 12 points
- Algebra: `3 × 2² = 12`

**Why It Works**:
- ✅ Visual pyramid building
- ✅ Concrete doubling
- ✅ Real cheer structure
- ✅ Teaches exponential growth

---

### 6. 🎵 **ROUTINE MASHUP** (Polynomials)
**Inspired by**: Cooking & Recipes (Scaling) with combining

**Real-World Scenario**:
> "Coach wants to combine TWO routines into ONE mega-routine! Routine A has 6 dance moves and 12 music beats. Routine B has 4 dance moves and 8 music beats. When you combine them, how many TOTAL moves and beats?"

**Algebra Taught**: `(6m + 12b) + (4m + 8b) = 10m + 20b`
- Combining like terms
- Addition of polynomials
- Grouping similar items

**Example**:
- Routine A: 6 moves, 12 beats
- Routine B: 4 moves, 8 beats
- Answer: 10 moves, 20 beats total
- Algebra: `6 + 4 = 10 moves`, `12 + 8 = 20 beats`

**Why It Works**:
- ✅ Real routine planning
- ✅ Concrete counts
- ✅ Visual combining
- ✅ Teaches like terms
